What does Drew Bledsoe do now?

Drew Bledsoe, the former New England Patriots quarterback, has led an eventful life after retiring from the NFL in 2007. Let's delve into what Drew Bledsoe is doing now and explore his current activities in detail.

Post-Retirement Settlement

After ending his 14-year NFL career, mostly spent with the New England Patriots, Bledsoe retired from the Dallas Cowboys and settled down in Bend, Oregon, with his wife, Maura, and their four children. Yet, retirement did not mark the end of his ventures.

Bledsoe has immersed himself in the world of winemaking, alongside his close friend Chris Figgins, by founding the Doubleback Winery in 2007.

Doubleback Winery

Bledsoe's focus has primarily been on the Doubleback Winery, where he and Maura bought a property on the southern border of the Walla Walla Valley AVA.

This estate-oriented winery prides itself on producing America's best Cabernet Sauvignon in an exquisite, true-to-varietal style.

Notably, the winery earned critical acclaim with its first vintage, securing a spot on the Wine Spectator's Top 100 List.

As a testament to his commitment, Bledsoe recruited Josh McDaniels, an essential member of the winery team, as the Director and CEO of Winemaking at Doubleback Winery.

Financial Success in NFL Career

According to Spotrac, Drew Bledsoe earned a staggering $80,056,250 during his NFL career, playing for the New England Patriots, Buffalo Bills, and Dallas Cowboys.

His performance on the field was equally impressive, leaving the league with 44,611 passing yards, 251 touchdowns, and 206 interceptions.

Legacy and Impact

Bledsoe's impact on the New England Patriots and the NFL at large has not been forgotten. Fans and analysts have reflected on his contributions, acknowledging his toughness, leadership, and instrumental role in turning the Patriots into winners.

While some have debated his statistical performance and compared him to other quarterbacks, it is evident that Bledsoe's legacy in the NFL remains a topic of interest and admiration among fans and enthusiasts.
