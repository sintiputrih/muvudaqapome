In Memoriam: Roland Leong – NHRA Pioneer, Team Owner, and the Hawaiian Racing Icon

The drag racing community is mourning the loss of a true legend with the passing of Roland Leong, the iconic NHRA team owner recognized for his Hawaiian-themed Top Fuel and Funny Cars.

Remembering Roland Leong

On the heels of a storied career in the National Hot Rod Association (NHRA), Roland Leong left an indelible mark on the world of drag racing.

Renowned for his Hawaiian-themed race cars and innovative approach, Leong’s legacy is etched in the annals of motorsports history. His recent passing at the age of 79 has prompted a collective reflection on the impact of “The Hawaiian” on the drag racing landscape.

Cause of Death

Despite the public’s admiration for Roland Leong’s achievements, the specifics surrounding his cause of death remain undisclosed.

Net Worth at the Time of Passing

As of 2024, Roland Leong’s reported net worth stands at $1.1 million.

This figure reflects not only his financial standing but also underscores the legacy of a man who invested his life in the pursuit of excellence in the drag racing world.

A Pioneer in NHRA

Roland Leong’s career saw him evolve from a driver to a team owner and crew chief, leaving an enduring impact on NHRA racing.

From his legendary victories to his role in shaping the sport’s landscape, Leong’s pioneering spirit blazed a trail for future generations of drag racing enthusiasts.
