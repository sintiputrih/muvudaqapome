Early Life and Career Beginnings

Jay Sean, born Kamaljit Singh Jhooti, began his journey in the London borough of Hounslow. His passion for music was evident from a young age, and he pursued it with vigor, eventually studying medicine before dropping out to follow his musical dreams. His early career was marked by a blend of R&B and Indian music, which quickly garnered him a following in the UK’s Asian Underground scene.

Breakthrough and International Success

Sean’s breakthrough came with his debut album “Me Against Myself” in 2004, which showcased his unique blend of R&B and South Asian influences. However, it was his move to Cash Money Records and the release of “Down” in 2009 that catapulted him to international fame. The single topped the Billboard Hot 100, making him the first solo artist of South Asian origin to do so.
