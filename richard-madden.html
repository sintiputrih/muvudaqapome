Richard Madden Biography

Richard Madden was born in Elderslie, Renfrewshire, Scotland, on June 18, 1986. He was born to Pat and Richard Madden. Professional Life & Career

Richard Madden began acting when he was a teenager. In 1999, his acting ability was recognized in BBC1’s “Barmy Aunt Boomerang.” Madden appeared in 20 episodes of the series and played an important part. In 2000, he made his feature film debut in the film adaptation of Iain Banks’ novel “Complicity.” Madden made his comeback as Dean McKenzie in the British TV comedy-drama series “Hope Springs” when he was 23 years old, after acting as a teenager.

He starred as Kirk Brandon in the television film ‘Worried About The Kid in 2010. After that, he played Ripley Collins in the suspense drama film ‘Chatroom,’ directed by Hideo Nakata, a well-known Japanese filmmaker. In 2011, he starred as Ashley Greenwich in the television series “Sirens” and then as Elliot in the short film “Strays.” Madden’s breakout role as Robb Stark in one of the most popular television series in the world, “Game of Thrones,” came the same year.

He is also known for his work in theatre, in addition to feature films and television shows. Madden collaborated with theatres such as the “Glasgow Repertory Company” and “The Archers” when he was a student.
