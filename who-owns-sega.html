Who Owns Sega? An In-Depth Look

Introduction

Sega is a video game company with a rich history dating back to the 1940s. Since its founding, it has grown to become a major player in the video game industry, known for iconic games such as Sonic the Hedgehog, Phantasy Star, and Yakuza. Over the years, there have been several changes in ownership of the company, causing many to wonder: who currently owns Sega?

A Brief History of Sega

Sega was founded in 1940 as a company that produced slot machines and jukeboxes. It wasn’t until the late 1950s that they began developing electronic games, and by the 1980s, they had become a major player in the arcade game industry. With the rise of home gaming consoles, Sega shifted its focus to the development of home consoles such as the Sega Genesis and the Dreamcast.

After several years of financial struggles and tough competition from other console manufacturers like Nintendo and Sony, Sega announced in 2001 that they would no longer be producing hardware and would instead focus solely on developing games.
