Arline Burks Gant Job

Arline Burks Gant is an actress, film director, fashion designer, and costume designer.

Her work in front of and behind the camera began as a cover girl for Magnificent Hair Products. During her teen years, she continued runway and print modeling. She started her career as a principal dancer in WEWS Channel 5’s Upbeat which was nationally syndicated from 1964 to 1971. The show was filmed entirely in her hometown of Cleveland, Ohio. This unique multicultural broadcast offered talent of all ages, an opportunity to promote and showcase their, dancing and musical talents nationwide.

Like many who participated in the show, her career escalated and she headed to Europe to work as a runway model for several couture houses.

According to IMDB, Arline also served as the director of several short films and TV shows such as Couture in the Extreme, 25 Lighthouse, Manners, and The Upbeat Dancer Show. 

The native of Cleveland launched her own clothing brand before opening a factory in Senegal, West Africa. Slowly, she shifted from directing and producing films to designing costumes. During this time, she also worked for Motown Records creating fashion concepts.

She worked in several productions such as Martin Lawrence Show, Daughters of the Dust, Deep Cover, Lovable Blond Seeking, AC Black, and many more. She even joked that her daughter started kindergarten on the Universal Studio lot.

In 1992, her work also earned her the accolade of Costume Designer of the Year for Theater.

Besides that, Arline is also the founder of several companies such as Dark P. Inc, P Dakota Inc, and For Women Like Me, Inc.,

She and her company The Dakar Group headed a project to bring a bit of Hollywood to Northeast Ohio in the form of a 140,000-square-foot production center named Dakar Studios. The center housed six film soundstages (one with a pool), four television production stages, four recording studios with a full editing bay, and a state-of-the-art animation studio.

“Ohio can mimic any space in the world for all types of production,” said Arline. “Anyone can come here and produce music, film, and TV. Ohio has so much to offer. The problem has been productions can only come here a few months out of the year because of weather. When we open, they can come here and stay to do post-production instead of running off to L.A. or New York.”

The estimated value of the project was $30 million- $40 million. She was also inducted into Bedford School’s hall of fame.
