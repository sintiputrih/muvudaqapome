What does Tteokbokki taste like?

My first taste of Korean food was tteokbokki – thick, chewy soft chubby rods of rice cakes enrobed in a spicy-salty-slightly sweet red pepper sauce.

Is Tteokbokki really spicy?

Tteokbokki is a Korean street food snack made primarily of chewy rice cakes and fiery, funky gochujang chili paste. Salty anchovy stock bolsters the gochujang and gochugaru chili flakes add even more heat. The final assemblage is unabashedly spicy, but impossible to put down.
