Holliday Grainger Biography

Holliday Grainger is an English screen and stage actress known for her roles in the BAFTA award-winning children’s series Roger and the in the Showtime series The Borgias, in Strike, in the BBC One crime drama The Capture and in Mike Newell’s adaptation of Great Expectations.

Grainger’s first acting role in the BBC comedy-drama series All Quiet on the Preston Front was at five years old. Roles in Casualty, Doctors, Dalziel, and Pascoe followed. Grainger has played in Where the Heart Is, in Waterloo Lane, and in Merlin, Sophia. She starred in tv series The Borgias in 2011. Created by Oscar-winning Neil Jordan and shot in Hungary, this series ran for three seasons.

World Refugee Day Grainger, as well as Jack O’Connell, appeared in a video from UNHCR’s refugee agency on 20 June 2016 to help raise awareness about the global refugee crisis. The movie, titled Home, has a family taking a reverse migration into a war zone midway through. Inspired by primary refugee accounts, and is part of UNHCR’s # WithRefugees campaign, which also includes a petition to governments to expand asylum to provide additional shelter, integrate job opportunities and education. Home, written and directed by Daniel Mulloy, won a BAFTA Award and a Gold Lion among many other awards at the Cannes Lions International Creativity Festival.
