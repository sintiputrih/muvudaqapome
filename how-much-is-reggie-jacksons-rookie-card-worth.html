How much is Reggie Jackson's rookie card worth?

Reggie Jackson, a legendary figure in the world of baseball, has left an indelible mark on the sport.

His rookie cards hold immense value among collectors, and understanding the factors contributing to their worth is crucial for any enthusiast.

Together with Fan Arch, we'll delve into the comprehensive details of the value associated with Reggie Jackson's rookie cards, focusing on factors such as rarity, condition, historical significance, and notable sales or auctions.

1969 Topps Reggie Jackson Rookie Card #260

The 1969 Topps Reggie Jackson rookie card #260 is an iconic piece sought after by collectors worldwide. Its value is influenced by various factors such as scarcity, condition, and historical significance.

Rarity and Supply

PSA has graded 10,711 of the 1969 Topps Reggie Jackson rookie cards, with only one card achieving gem mint 10 status. This rarity contributes significantly to its value, making it a prized possession among collectors.

Historical Significance

Reggie Jackson's status as a Hall of Famer and his popularity as one of the most iconic players of his time add immense historical significance to this rookie card. The card captures the essence of Jackson's flair for the dramatic, making it a truly coveted item among enthusiasts.

Sales and Auctions

Notable sales of the 1969 Topps Reggie Jackson rookie card include a PSA 9 example sold for a staggering $84,000 by Robert Edward Auctions in April 2022, highlighting the tremendous worth attributed to this card.

1970 Topps Reggie Jackson #140

The 1970 Topps Reggie Jackson card #140 is another valuable piece that holds significance in the world of baseball card collecting.

Rarity and Supply

PSA has graded 3,809 of the 1970 Topps Reggie Jackson cards, with only one achieving gem mint 10 status. This limited supply contributes to its desirability and value among collectors.

Sales and Auctions

An example of the 1970 Topps Reggie Jackson card #140 in PSA 9 condition was sold for $3,360 at a Heritage Auctions event in July 2023, further highlighting its substantial worth in the market.

1971, 1972, 1973, and 1977 Topps Reggie Jackson Cards

Aside from his rookie card, Reggie Jackson's other cards from subsequent years also hold significant value and are sought after by collectors.

Rarity and Sales

The rarity and condition of these cards play a pivotal role in determining their worth. For instance, the 1977 Topps Reggie Jackson #10 card, with a price range of $5 to $8,157, is highly valued due to its stunning design, rarity, and historical significance.

Factors Affecting Card Value

Condition

The condition of a card significantly impacts its value. Cards in gem mint condition command the highest prices due to their pristine state, while factors such as centering, print defects, and overall appearance influence the card's desirability and worth.

Historical Significance

Cards associated with significant milestones in a player's career or those capturing iconic moments hold substantial historical significance, contributing to their value in the market.

Rarity and Scarcity

Limited supply, especially in high-grade condition, greatly elevates the value of baseball cards. Rare cards with fewer examples in top condition are highly sought after by collectors, leading to increased worth in the market.

 

All in all, Reggie Jackson's rookie card, along with subsequent cards from other years, holds immense value in the world of baseball card collecting.

The rarity, condition, historical significance, and notable sales or auctions all contribute to the substantial worth of these cards.

As a result, they remain highly sought after by enthusiasts and collectors, solidifying their status as prized possessions in the realm of sports memorabilia.
