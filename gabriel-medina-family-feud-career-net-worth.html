Gabriel Medina: Early life, Family, and Education

Medina was born to Simone Pinto Medina and Claudio de Jesus Ferreira. He was born in the city of Sao Paulo.

Gabriel was hardworking and focused on surfing from his childhood.  

He found his passion and started surfing at the age of 9 after his parents got divorced.

His mother Simone and stepfather Charles Serrano de Saldanha Rodrigues raised him.

In addition, Charles is also the role model and surfing coach of Gabriel Medina.

Similarly, he has a brother, Felipe, and half-sister, Sophia Rodrigues Medina. 

After two years of surfing, he won his first national championship in Buzios, Rio de Janeiro.

At the age of 14, the surfer has already taken part in the World Qualifying Series. In addition, he was the finalist of the Paulista Championship.

Famous Rip Curl contracted Medina at the age of 15 in July 2009.

Immediately, he becomes the youngest surfer ever to win in the World Surf League in the category of Men’s qualifying event.

Gabriel Medina: Career and Achievements

Brazilian Gabriel Medina is one of the most popular surfers of the 21st century.

Medina started his professional surfing career at the very young age of 13 with the sponsorship of Rip Curl.

However, he became famous at the age of 11 after he won his first title.

The flip at the Wall in Hawaii in 2012 was the sensational moment in his career with his creative aerialist.

Moreover, Medina was crowned with ASP World Junior Championship in 2013.

Brazil’s popular surfer joined WSL World Tour at the age of 17.

Three years later, Gabriel won his first World surfing Champion. This is the historic moment for Gabriel and Brazilian surfers. 

Similarly, Medina became the youngest world champion tying Kelly Slater at the age of 20. He is celebrated as the national sports star.

Moreover, this success brought him an endorsement from mainstream companies like Mitsubishi and Samsung.

He is called “the Brazilian Strom” by thousands of dedicated surfers.

Gabriel is a surfing star and one of the highest-paid surfers with a large fan following.

In addition, he became the first Brazilian to win the prestigious Triple Crown of surfing in 2015.

Through all the thick and thin in his career, Medina grabbed his second world title in 2018. 

Moreover, the surfer also claimed Billabong Pipe Master in the same year.

Recently, in 2021, the most popular surfer represented Brazil at the Tokyo 2020 Olympic Games, where he lost a bronze medal match with Owen Wright.

The Gabriel Medina Institute

Gabriel Medina and his family founded the Instituto Gabriel Medina (IGM) in 2017. It is the training center in Maresias, Brazil. 

In addition, Medina is using the institute as the medium to breed the next World Champions.

This institute is built in the place where Gabriel Medina learned to surf.

Moreover, IGM has become the reference for young surfers in Brazil.

In fact, Instituto Gabriel Medina was founded after Gabriel clinched his first World title.

Gabriel thinks of IGM as one of his achievements. In addition, he wants to produce World Champion from Brazil.
